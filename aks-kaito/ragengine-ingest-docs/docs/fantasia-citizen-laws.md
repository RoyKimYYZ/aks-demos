# Fantasia Citizen Code (FCC)

This document is a **work of fiction**. It describes the laws of the fantasy island jurisdiction known as **Fantasia**.

It is intentionally written with **unique names, agencies, places, and rule numbers** to support demonstrations of:
- document ingestion into a vector database

# Title I — Citizenship & Civic Duties

## §1.01 Citizenship classes
1. **Resident Citizen**: a person registered in the Fantasia Civic Ledger for at least 180 days in a rolling 12-month period.
2. **Harbor Citizen**: a person who works in Fantasia’s harbor districts for at least 90 days per year and holds a Harbor Badge.
3. **Shore Visitor**: any non-citizen present for fewer than 180 days.

## §1.02 Civic Ledger registration
1. Every Resident Citizen must register an address within one of Fantasia’s recognized boroughs: **Luminara**, **Coralstead**, **Northwind Quay**, **Verdant Crown**, or **Glassbay**.
2. Updates to address must be submitted within **10 days**.
3. The Civic Ledger is maintained by the **Bureau of Lanterns and Records (BLR)**.

## §1.03 Civic Duty Days
1. Each Resident Citizen must complete **two Civic Duty Days** per calendar year.
2. Civic Duty Days may be satisfied by:
   - coastal cleanup programs
   - library steward shifts
   - trail maintenance in Verdant Crown
   - volunteer staffing at the annual **Moonkite Festival**
3. Exemptions are available for:
   - medical hardship certified by a licensed healer
   - caretaking responsibilities for dependents under 5 years
   - citizens over 72 years

## §1.04 Civic Courtesy Rule
1. A citizen must, when reasonably safe, offer assistance to a person in **clear distress** (lost child, injured traveler, etc.).
2. The duty is satisfied by contacting a public responder (Harbor Watch, Lantern Wardens) when direct aid is not safe.

# Title II — Public Order & Community Standards

## §2.01 Quiet Lantern Hours
1. Quiet Lantern Hours are **21:30–06:30** daily.
2. During Quiet Lantern Hours, amplified sound is prohibited within:
   - 150 meters of hospitals and carehouses
   - 80 meters of residential blocks
   - all public libraries
3. Exceptions:
   - emergency announcements
   - sanctioned festivals (permit required under §7.08)

## §2.02 Festival Permits
1. Public gatherings exceeding **120 persons** require a Festival Permit issued by the **Lantern Wardens (LW)**.
2. Permit applications must include:
   - a safety plan
   - a waste plan
   - a water access plan if near shorelines

## §2.03 Street Trade Etiquette
1. Street vendors must keep a **two-step passage lane** clear on sidewalks.
2. Vendors must provide a visible placard indicating:
   - pricing in Fantasian Shellmarks (FSM)
   - allergen disclosures for food items

## §2.04 Prohibition on “False Beaconing”
1. A person must not display, project, or broadcast a **false distress beacon**.
2. “False distress beacon” includes any signal that reasonably imitates:
   - harbor emergency lantern codes
   - official storm-warning patterns
   - medical urgency beacons
3. This section does not prohibit theatrical performance when clearly labeled as fiction and restricted to authorized venues.

# Title III — Commerce, Trade, and Fair Dealing

## §3.01 Honest Measures Act
1. Sellers must not use altered scales, rigged measuring cups, or misleading volume markings.
2. The **Office of Fair Shellmarks (OFS)** may conduct surprise inspections.

## §3.02 The Three-Price Rule
1. A merchant must not display more than **three distinct prices** for the same good in the same storefront window.
2. If multiple prices are displayed, the **lowest** displayed price is enforceable.

## §3.03 Returns for “Glowglass” goods
1. “Glowglass” goods (lamps, ornaments, panels) must be returnable within **14 days** if:
   - the glow fails under normal indoor use
   - the item was sold as “stable-glow”
2. Returns may be denied if the buyer intentionally exposed Glowglass to:
   - salt-spray immersion
   - forge heat
   - direct sky-lane exhaust

## §3.04 Anti-Fraud: Mirage Sales
1. “Mirage sale” means selling a good or service using deceptive imagery, staged scarcity, or fabricated testimonials.
2. Mirage sales are prohibited in:
   - physical markets
   - traveling caravans
   - digital storefronts hosted within Fantasia (§5.02)

## §3.05 Apprenticeship fairness
1. Apprentices must receive:
   - a written agreement
   - a weekly rest day
   - training hours that exceed menial tasks by a meaningful portion
2. The **Guild Council of Nine Trades (GCNT)** publishes apprenticeship guidelines every 2 years.

# Title IV — Environment, Waterways, and Sky-Lanes

## §4.01 Coral Sanctuary Zones
1. Coral Sanctuary Zones include:
   - **Azure Ring Reef**
   - **Whisper Shoals**
   - **Kelp Cathedral**
2. Within Coral Sanctuary Zones, it is prohibited to:
   - anchor a vessel except at marked anchors
   - harvest coral, shells attached to coral, or reef fish
   - discharge waste of any kind

## §4.02 Freshwater Stewardship
1. Freshwater reservoirs are designated **Blue Wells**.
2. Greywater discharge within 300 meters of a Blue Well is prohibited.
3. The **Verdant Crown Waterkeepers (VCW)** may issue emergency restrictions during drought.

## §4.03 Sky-Lane Safety Corridors
1. Fantasia maintains regulated aerial corridors called **Sky-Lanes**.
2. Personal gliders, kiteboards, and courier drones must not enter a Sky-Lane without:
   - a Sky-Lane Token (renewed annually)
   - a visible registration mark
3. During the **Moonkite Festival week**, Sky-Lanes above Luminara are closed from 16:00–23:00.

## §4.04 Lanternfish Protection
1. Lanternfish are a protected species.
2. It is prohibited to:
   - capture Lanternfish
   - sell Lanternfish or Lanternfish oil
   - illuminate Lanternfish spawning coves at night
3. Scientific research permits may be granted by the **Council of Tides and Light (CTL)**.

# Title V — Digital Conduct & Data Stewardship

## §5.01 The Courteous Circuit Standard
1. Digital services offered to Fantasian citizens must provide:
   - a clear purpose statement (“why data is collected”)
   - a simple method to correct inaccurate records
   - a contact channel answered within **12 business days**

## §5.02 Fantasia-Hosted Services Registry
1. Any service hosted on Fantasia soil or under Fantasia domain registry must register with the **Registry of Shoreline Systems (RSS)**.
2. Registration requires:
   - a security contact
   - an incident notification address
   - an annual “Storm Drill” participation (tabletop or technical exercise)

## §5.03 Prohibition on “Glass-ID Harvesting”
1. “Glass-ID” means a Fantasian identity token used for civic services.
2. It is prohibited to:
   - request a Glass-ID outside of approved authentication windows
   - store a Glass-ID in plaintext
   - sell or trade Glass-ID values

## §5.04 Breach notification
1. When a service operator reasonably believes that citizen data has been exposed, the operator must:
   - notify RSS within **72 hours**
   - notify affected citizens within **7 days** if the exposure is likely to cause harm
2. Notification must include:
   - what happened
   - what data was involved
   - what citizens can do
   - what remediation steps were taken

## §5.05 Automated decision transparency
1. If a service uses automated rules to:
   - deny access to a service
   - prioritize citizens for a scarce resource
   - assign a “risk score”
   it must provide a plain-language explanation upon request.

# Title VI — Transportation & Harbor Rules

## §6.01 Harbor Safety Lantern Codes
1. Harbor signals use official lantern codes maintained by the **Harbor Watch (HW)**.
2. Unauthorized use of official lantern code patterns is prohibited (see also §2.04).

## §6.02 Docking priority
1. Docking priority order in emergencies:
   1) medical transport skiffs
   2) waterkeeper vessels
   3) supply barges (food/medicine)
   4) all other vessels

## §6.03 Sandpath right-of-way
1. On marked sandpaths, the right-of-way order is:
   1) mobility carts
   2) pedestrians
   3) bicycles
   4) small motor scooters
2. Maximum scooter speed on sandpaths is **12 knots equivalent** (posted areas may be lower).

## §6.04 Night sailing restrictions
1. Night sailing is restricted within 2 nautical miles of Whisper Shoals unless:
   - the vessel is equipped with a certified low-glare lantern
   - the operator holds a Night Skipper Stamp

# Title VII — Dispute Resolution & Enforcement

## §7.01 Agencies and jurisdiction
Primary agencies include:
- **Bureau of Lanterns and Records (BLR)** — Civic Ledger and citizen registration
- **Lantern Wardens (LW)** — permits, public order, festival safety
- **Harbor Watch (HW)** — harbor enforcement and emergency response
- **Verdant Crown Waterkeepers (VCW)** — freshwater protection and drought measures
- **Office of Fair Shellmarks (OFS)** — commerce inspections and fraud enforcement
- **Registry of Shoreline Systems (RSS)** — digital services registry and incident notifications
- **Council of Tides and Light (CTL)** — environmental protections and research permits

## §7.02 Penalty categories
Penalties in Fantasia are categorized as:
- **Level A**: warning or education order
- **Level B**: administrative fine
- **Level C**: fine plus temporary permit suspension
- **Level D**: license/permit revocation and community service

(See Annex B for typical amounts.)

## §7.03 Due process and appeals
1. A cited person must receive a written notice describing:
   - the alleged violation
   - evidence collected
   - how to appeal
2. Appeals may be filed within **21 days** to the **Civic Review Bench (CRB)**.
3. CRB hearings may be in-person or via secure circuit session.

## §7.04 Evidence retention
1. Enforcement agencies must retain evidence for:
   - 1 year for Level A and Level B
   - 3 years for Level C and Level D
2. Evidence may include:
   - photographs of signage or rules
   - vessel registration snapshots
   - logs from authorized sensors (e.g., Sky-Lane monitors)

## §7.05 Community restoration orders
When a violation harms the community or environment, agencies may order restoration actions, such as:
- shoreline cleanup hours
- reef stewardship contributions
- public apology postings for Mirage Sales violations

## §7.06 Special protection for minors
1. For persons under 16, enforcement prioritizes education and guardian notification.
2. Monetary fines should be converted into guardian responsibility or community service.

## §7.07 Permit suspension and reinstatement
1. Suspended permits may be reinstated when:
   - fines are paid or waived
   - required training is completed
   - an inspection passes (where applicable)

## §7.08 Festival Permit schedule
1. Festival Permit fees depend on attendance forecast:
   - up to 120 persons: no permit (but notice required)
   - 121–400 persons: standard permit
   - 401+ persons: enhanced permit with safety marshal
2. The Lantern Wardens publish an updated fee schedule annually.

# Annex A — Defined Terms

**Blue Wells**: protected freshwater reservoirs used for drinking and civic supply.

**Civic Duty Day**: an approved volunteer day recorded in the Civic Ledger.

**Fantasia Shellmark (FSM)**: Fantasia’s official currency unit for fines and fees.

**False Beaconing**: displaying or broadcasting distress signals that imitate official emergency codes.

**Glass-ID**: the official identity token used for Fantasia civic and public services.

**Glowglass**: a decorative and functional glass material sold with glow stability claims.

**Mirage Sale**: a sales practice involving deceptive imagery, staged scarcity, or fabricated testimonials.

**Quiet Lantern Hours**: nightly quiet period from 21:30 to 06:30.

**Sky-Lane**: regulated aerial corridor reserved for authorized aerial traffic.

# Annex B — Schedules (Fees and Penalties)

These are typical penalty amounts. Agencies may adjust based on circumstances.

- **Level A**: written warning; optional training module “Courteous City, Courteous Shore”
- **Level B**: 25–180 FSM
- **Level C**: 200–900 FSM + suspension of relevant permit for 7–30 days
- **Level D**: 1000–4500 FSM + revocation for 90–365 days + 10–60 hours community restoration

**Common examples**
- §2.01 Quiet Lantern Hours violation (first offense): Level A or Level B (25–60 FSM)
- §2.04 False Beaconing: Level C or Level D depending on response triggered
- §4.01 Coral Sanctuary anchoring (unmarked anchor): Level C (300–900 FSM)
- §5.03 Glass-ID Harvesting: Level D (revocation + restoration order)
